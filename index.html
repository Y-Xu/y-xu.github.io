Yang Xu is currently an Associate Professor at the School of Computer Science and Technology, University of Science and Technology of China (USTC), and a core member of the USTC-INT Group. He received his B.S. degree from Wuhan University of Technology in 2014 and his Ph.D. degree in computer science and technology from USTC in 2019. His research spans Artificial Intelligence of Things (AIoT), edge intelligence, federated learning, with an emphasis on architecting synergistic frameworks that unify edge computing and AI. His current work pioneers federated LLMs and goal-oriented AI agents to enable secure, decentralized intelligent systems with human-in-the-loop adaptability

‌Dr. Xu spearheads a multidisciplinary team dedicated to redefining "device-edge-cloud" collaborative intelligence. He actively seeks motivated students passionate about pushing boundaries in edge-AI frameworks, self-adaptive agent ecosystems, and trustworthy federated learning architectures. The group emphasizes interdisciplinary collaboration to deliver groundbreaking research with real-world impact, nurturing future innovators to tackle challenges in smart healthcare, industrial IoT, and human-centric AI agents.

‌Prospective members are expected to thrive in a rigorous yet supportive environment, combining theoretical rigor with hands-on system development to build robust edge-AI prototypes, ultimately contributing to trustworthy autonomous systems for our hyper-connected world
